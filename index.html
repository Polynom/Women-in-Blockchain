The project

IAMANEH (International Association for Maternal and Neonatal Health) was founded in 1978 by the Swiss Gynecologist Hubert de Watteville and is a ZEWO certified charity. It focuses on the physical, social and psychological well-being of women and children in Mali, Senegal, Togo, Burkina Faso, Bosnia-Herzegovina and Albania. The “Token of Gratitude” community fundraiser supports a 3-year project (2016-2019) in Burkina Faso which the charity implements together with their local partner AMMIE (Association pour l’Appui Moral, Matériel et Intellectuel de l’Enfant). The focus of their effort is to provide young women with information about and access to reliable means of family planning. Teenage pregnancies are often the start of a vicious circle which leads to young women being rejected from their communities, failing to complete schooling and ending up in prostitution or other dependencies. IAMANEH together with AMMIE provide schooling for “peer-educators”, over 100 young women and men who engage with their local communities on issues around the topic of family planning. They also run health centers where young women can access information, medical support and contraceptive means. Lastly, IAMANEH supports young women with children in building up their own source of income, e.g. a sewing or barber shop, to enable their financial independence.

Our cause

As part of our commitment to equal opportunities for everyone, Trust Square together with Procivis and FDP Women Switzerland launched a community fundraiser to benefit young women in Ouahigouya, Burkina Faso. For this purpose, we have built a charity smart contract called “Token of Gratitude” based on which individuals can contribute Ether to the project. In return, the smart contract will issue a token to the contributor’s wallet which will serve as a “donation certificate”. The smart contract will run for a total of 8 weeks (August 7, 2018). After this, the funds collected will be released to the wallet of the charity. You can find the repository of the smart contract here.

We believe that fundraising via public ledger can greatly increase transparency and reduce administrative costs for charitable giving. We are very happy that the Swiss charity IAMANEH agreed to collaborate with us to accept donations denominated in Ether.
